You won't find our beers under a multitude of different names.

Rather we brew the following styles, and each time we'll change them up slightly.

That might be different hops, or a different adjunct added.

Pale & Hoppy

Pale Ale

A Pale Ale brewed with various hops, flavour profile is subtly different depending on what hops were used. Around 5.5% ABV. Please drink fresh.

Dark Beers

Double India Porter

A roastier, hoppier and stronger version of our Export India Porter. Lots of bitterness and intensity.

Purchase Here
Dry Stout

A dry stout - roasty and round and hop forward. Hop varieties change with each batch.

Purchase Here
Export India Porter

Inspired by the porter recipes of the 1850s that were sent to India, with modern hopping regimes. Hops change from batch to batch. Notes of chocolate, coffee, tobacco, leather. ABV always around 6%.

Purchase Here
Export Stout

Our version of a 130 year old, Export Stout recipe from an old London brewery. We have not changed the recipe much. It's roasty, toasty, bitter, chocolatey, with notes of leather, tar, tobacco, deep plum and raisins. from the signature London brown malt.

Purchase Here
Imperial Brown Ale

Brewed to and 1856 recipe from the Barclay Perkins brewery. Rich dark treacle, cut with coffee and chocolate bitterness, and then also that of the hops. Again the brown and amber malts come through to play with smoke, tar, leather and depth.

Purchase Here

Sours & Saisons

Foeder Beer

Our take on a Belgian Pale Ale, fermented with a blend of belgian pale ale yeast, saison yeast, and our house mixed culture, in one of our foeders. Lovely complex yeast character, bitterness and fruitiness from the hops, a touch of acidity from our house culture, with the foeder seeming to give a lovely texture to the whole thing.

Purchase Here
Bière de Saison

Our Bière de Saison is fermented with our house mixed culture of yeast, wild yeast and bacteria, and develops into a dry, effervescent and tart beer after a bit of time. We then either age it in old ex wine barrels / puncheons / foudres for 8-9 months and often dry hop it, or we use this beer as a base for refermentation with fruit, as the seasons permit.

Purchase Here
