Our version of a 130 year old, Export Stout recipe from an old London brewery. We have not changed the recipe much. It's roasty, toasty, bitter, chocolatey, with notes of leather, tar, tobacco, deep plum and raisins. from the signature London brown malt.

Brewed to and 1856 recipe from the Barclay Perkins brewery. Rich dark treacle, cut with coffee and chocolate bitterness, and then also that of the hops. Again the brown and amber malts come through to play with smoke, tar, leather and depth.

Sours & Saisons

Our take on a Belgian Pale Ale, fermented with a blend of belgian pale ale yeast, saison yeast, and our house mixed culture, in one of our foeders. Lovely complex yeast character, bitterness and fruitiness from the hops, a touch of acidity from our house culture, with the foeder seeming to give a lovely texture to the whole thing.
